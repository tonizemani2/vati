★Importance scoring — how a concept earns depth

Importance is not a fabricated number and not a momentum proxy — it's an AI reasoning task (Claude in-session) scored toward the thesis: how plausibly is this concept a future locus of constraint migration with a real mechanism, and is it still pre-consensus? Every score carries a rationale (GIGO). Running this prompt over the inventory fills the ▢ Importance / Depth columns and drives curation.

▶The scoring prompt · feed one concept at a time, store the JSON[~] ready to run
CONCEPT IMPORTANCE SCORING — constraint-migration foresight FRAME. You are scoring one technology/research concept for a foresight engine whose only goal is to find, BEFORE the market prices it, where scarcity and value will migrate. Thesis: rent accrues to the binding constraint — value lands where accelerating capability collides with slow-moving supply (a fab, a mineral, a skill, a permit, a scarce input). A concept matters to THIS system in proportion to how plausibly it becomes a future locus of constraint migration WITH A REAL MECHANISM — not how exciting, famous, or fast-growing it is. STANCE (method, not answers). Be a fox: many lenses, lightly held. Distrust clean narratives and your own first take. Anchor to base rates. Mechanism-free momentum is the fizzle signature — discount it. Penalize consensus darlings (already priced = no edge). INPUT. A concept name, optionally its works/year acceleration (σ) and domain. Treat acceleration as ONE weak signal — publication growth ≠ value migration. SCORE each sub-dimension 0–100, each with a one-line justification (no naked numbers): 1. constraint_migration — if this capability keeps improving, does it create/shift a BINDING constraint where rent would concentrate? Name where the bottleneck lands. 2. mechanism — is there a real, nameable driver (Wright's-law cost decline, a physical/ biological limit being crossed, a hard input)? Or mechanism-free momentum? (low = fizzle risk) 3. value_at_stake — order-of-magnitude economic value & breadth if the migration happens. 4. pre_consensus — how likely this is NOT already priced in / widely anticipated. (darling = low) 5. forecastability — can we state a falsifiable, point-in-time-checkable claim with a resolution date + kill-criteria? (unfalsifiable = low) THEN: importance 0–100 aggregate. constraint_migration & mechanism dominate; pre_consensus is a strong multiplier.
